A Brief history

Handheld consoles have been a popular method for bringing games with you wherever you want. There has been a lot of development in how much the technology inside of these consoles and there have been multiple companies who have attempted to create their own consoles. Some of these include Nintendo, Sony, Sega, and Valve.

  • Early handhelds

    • Early handhelds were often small and in many cases had only one game on them. These consoles would look simiar to the Game and Watches from Nintendo and as time went on they started to become more advanved with the Wonder Swan and the Atari Lynx. Not many of these would catch on however.

  • Nintendo

    • Nintendo has been the longest Competitor in the world of handheld game consoles starting with their Game And Watch systems which only had one game until they made the Gameboy. After the GameBoy and the subsequent Gameboy Color, Nintendo would release the Gameboy Advance system. Following this the Nintendo DS was launched followed by the 3DS. After the 3DS Nintendo would merge their efforts in home consoles and handheld consoles to create the first ever "hybrid" console the Nintendo Swich. Which will soon be followed by the Nintendo Switch 2.

  • Sony

    • Sony had a short time in the handheld world first releasing the PSP (Play Station Portable) which would be followed by the PSVita years later. Sony has not released another handheld since the Vita in 2011.

  • Sega

    • Sega tried their hand at creating handheld consoles as well during their reign of consoles. The first was the Game Gear released in 1990. Soon after the Sega Nomad was released in 95. Sega would not have another handheld game console release after the Nomad.

  • Computer Handhelds

    • Computer handhelds have continuously cropped up over the last few years. The first of which was the Steam Deck from Valve. After this consoles like the ROG Ally, Lenovo Legion Go, and the MSI Claw. Most of these are handheld Windows machines while the SteamDeck is the only one with an OS that is run inside of Linux. This form of handhelds is still relitively new and quite expensive compared to others on the market.
